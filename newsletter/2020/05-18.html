Letter from a code editor

Dear readers,

Thank you for your interest in the Operation Spark Alumni Newsletter! In this newsletter we aim to share resources that we think are useful, interesting, or just awesome. This month’s content comes from the alumni board, but if you read to the end we have a form for alum suggestions! Community

TypeScript Discord

A great way to get into a new language or framework is to dive into a community surrounding your subject of interest. This month we have the TypeScript Discord, for our JS fans who want to keep things organized with types (and other cool features). With over 5000 members, and a fifth of them currently online as I’m typing this, you’re bound to see some great conversations around this JavaScript superset.

Podcast

Working Smarter in Your Creative Business

Though this podcast is not about development, it’s about design, the idea of working smarter can translate into any field of work. I really loved listening to the hosts speak about improving yourself, or your work, by just 1% each day. It’s such an attainable goal, and though it feels small in the moment it leads to much larger gains.

Inspirational Software Project

Zelda Video Game Randomizer

This is a cohesive and fairly lightweight open-source Python program that does the arcane magic of taking the tiny ROM of The Legend of Zelda for the NES/Famicom, shuffling things around such that the game is still completable with a variety of configurations, and producing a new ROM of a unique The Legend of Zelda experience. Many video game randomizers are closed-source, so this is a fun and uncommon opportunity to dig into the kind of thing that makes many people’s passion projects!

Suggested by Alexa
